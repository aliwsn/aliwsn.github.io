How is Depression diagnosed

Most people have felt sad or depressed at times. Feeling depressed can be a normal reaction to loss, life's struggles . But when feelings of intense sadness -- including feeling helpless, hopeless, and worthless ,last for many days to weeks and keep you from functioning normally. our depression may be something more than sadness. It may very well be clinical depression (a treatable medical condition).

    

Usually what causes depression  are Personal factors,like:

Family history – Depression can run in families and some people will be at an increased genetic risk.

Personality – Some people may be more at risk of depression because of their personality, particularly if they have a tendency to worry a lot, have low self-esteem.

Serious medical illness – The stress and worry of coping with a serious illness can lead to depression, especially if you’re dealing with long-term management.

Drug and alcohol use – Drug and alcohol use can both lead to and result from depression. Many people with depression also have drug and alcohol problems.



How is Depression Diagnosed? 

Depression or depressive disorder is a syndrome that reflects a sad mood exceeding normal sadness or grief.
